\# 15 — RG22 Constitutional Processes



\## Purpose



This document defines the Constitutional Process Model used by the Living Constitutional World.



Constitutional Processes are the mechanisms that transform constitutional world state over time.



Every change to the world originates from one or more Constitutional Processes.



\## Constitutional Principle



The world does not evolve through isolated events.



The world evolves through continuously executing Constitutional Processes operating upon constitutional state.



\## Constitutional Process



A Constitutional Process is a deterministic transformation that evaluates the current world revision and produces a new constitutional revision.



Processes never modify history.



Processes produce the next constitutional state.

```text

Rain

&#x20;   ↓
```

\## Governance Integration



Every Constitutional Process remains subject to constitutional governance.



A process may:



\- Execute

\- Be delayed

\- Be partially executed

\- Be constitutionally denied



Process execution is therefore governed rather than assumed.



\## World Revision



Every completed Constitutional Process contributes to a new constitutional world revision.



The revised world becomes the constitutional basis for future simulation.



\## Evidence



Every Constitutional Process produces replayable evidence describing:



\- Input revision

\- Dependencies

\- Governance outcome

\- State transformation

\- Output revision



\## Architectural Properties



The Constitutional Process Model guarantees:



\- deterministic transformation

\- constitutional governance

\- dependency ordering

\- replayable evidence

\- reproducible world evolution



\## Summary



Constitutional Processes are the engine of emergence within the Living Constitutional World.



They transform constitutional state through governed, deterministic, and replayable world evolution while preserving constitutional integrity across successive revisions.
