\# 26 — RG22 Environmental Model



\## Purpose



This document defines the Constitutional Environmental Model for the Constitutional Game Runtime.



The Environmental Model represents the governed physical state of the world.



Unlike traditional game engines, environmental state is not cosmetic. It is constitutional state that participates directly in execution admissibility, world evolution, and constitutional governance.



\---



\# Constitutional Principle



The environment is a first-class constitutional subsystem.



Every environmental condition exists as governed state capable of influencing every other constitutional subsystem.



Environmental state evolves continuously through admissible constitutional processes.



\---



\# Constitutional Environmental State



The Environmental Model consists of the following canonical state.



```text

Environmental State

├── Season

├── Weather

├── Climate

├── Temperature

├── Rainfall

├── Water Availability

├── Soil Fertility

├── Wind

├── Natural Events

├── Environmental Revision

└── Environmental Evidence

```



Each component is governed independently while remaining part of a unified constitutional state.

\# Initial Environmental Variables



Revision 1 introduces the following governed variables.



\## Season



Represents the long-term temporal cycle.



Examples:



\- Spring

\- Summer

\- Autumn

\- Winter



\---



\## Weather



Represents short-term atmospheric conditions.



Examples:



\- Clear

\- Cloudy

\- Rain

\- Storm

\- Snow



\---



\## Climate



Represents persistent regional environmental characteristics.



Examples:



\- Temperate

\- Tropical

\- Desert

\- Arctic

\- Oceanic



\---



\## Temperature



Represents current environmental temperature affecting biological and resource processes.



\---



\## Rainfall



Represents water entering the environment through precipitation.



\---



\## Water Availability



Represents the constitutional supply of usable environmental water.



\---



\## Soil Fertility



Represents long-term agricultural productivity.



\---



\## Wind



Represents atmospheric movement influencing weather and future transportation systems.



\---



\## Natural Events



Represents discrete environmental occurrences including:



\- Flood

\- Drought

\- Wildfire

\- Blizzard

\- Hurricane

\- Earthquake



Future reference generations may expand this catalog.



\---



\# Constitutional Evolution



Environmental state evolves continuously.



Each simulation tick may modify one or more environmental variables through constitutional environmental processes.



Environmental evolution remains deterministic under identical constitutional conditions.



\---



\# Constitutional Evidence



Every environmental revision records:



\- Previous environmental state

\- Updated environmental state

\- Executed environmental processes

\- Denied environmental processes

\- Constitutional revision

\- Environmental evidence



Environmental evidence becomes part of the immutable constitutional history.

\# Summary



The Constitutional Environmental Model establishes a governed environmental state that continuously evolves through constitutional processes.



Rather than serving as background simulation, the environment becomes an active constitutional participant whose state directly influences admissibility, resource availability, biological evolution, settlement development, economic production, and governance across the entire Constitutional Runtime.
