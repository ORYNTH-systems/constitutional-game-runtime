\# 24 — CP-006 Population Mortality



\## Purpose



This document defines Constitutional Process CP-006: Population Mortality.



Population Mortality reduces population when constitutional conditions indicate sustained biological failure.



\## Constitutional Principle



Mortality is not random.



Mortality is an admissible constitutional consequence of accumulated stress, degraded health, and insufficient resources.



\## Inputs



CP-006 evaluates:



\- Current population

\- Population health

\- Hunger pressure

\- Food availability

\- Current world revision



\## Outputs



CP-006 may update:



\- Total population



\## Governance Conditions



Population mortality is admissible when one or more conditions are satisfied:



\- Population Health ≤ 25

\- Hunger Pressure ≥ 10

\- Food Consumption has been repeatedly denied



If these conditions are not met, mortality is constitutionally denied.



\## Evidence



Each execution records:



\- previous population

\- updated population

\- health

\- hunger pressure

\- food availability

\- governance result
