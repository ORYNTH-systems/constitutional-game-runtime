\# 36 — EP-005 Soil Fertility



\## Purpose



This document defines Environmental Process EP-005: Soil Fertility.



EP-005 reconstructs constitutional soil fertility from environmental state, completing the first ecological dependency chain within the Constitutional Game Runtime.



\---



\# Constitutional Principle



Soil fertility is not a fixed environmental attribute.



It is governed constitutional state reconstructed from upstream environmental conditions.



\---



\# Inputs



EP-005 evaluates:



\- Climate

\- Season

\- Weather

\- Water Availability

\- Temperature

\- Rainfall

\- Current Soil Fertility



\---



\# Outputs



EP-005 may update:



\- Soil Fertility

\- Environmental Evidence

\- Environmental Revision

\# Initial Deterministic Rules



Revision 1 defines deterministic reconstruction.



\### Abundant Water



Increase soil fertility.



\### Normal Water



Maintain soil fertility.



\### Limited Water



Reduce soil fertility.



\### Scarce Water



Reduce soil fertility more rapidly.



\---



\# Constitutional Soil States



Revision 1 models fertility numerically from:



```text

0–100

```



Future generations may introduce richer soil chemistry while preserving constitutional execution.



\---



\# Governance



Execution is admissible when:



\- Water Availability is valid.

\- Climate is valid.

\- Weather is valid.



Otherwise execution is denied.

EP-005 establishes soil fertility as a governed environmental resource derived from constitutional state rather than static configuration.



This completes Environmental System Version 1.0 and prepares Food Growth to become environmentally driven in the next reference generation.
