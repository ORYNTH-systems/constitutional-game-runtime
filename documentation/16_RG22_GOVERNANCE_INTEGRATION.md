\# 16 — RG22 Governance Integration



\## Purpose



This document defines how constitutional governance integrates with world evolution.



Governance is not an isolated subsystem.



It is continuously embedded within every Constitutional Process.



\## Constitutional Principle



Every transformation of constitutional state must remain constitutionally admissible.



No process, event, or autonomous behavior bypasses governance.



Governance evaluates every proposed state transition before it becomes part of the constitutional world.

\## Governance Responsibilities



Governance continuously evaluates:



\- Constitutional constraints

\- Resource sufficiency

\- Population conditions

\- Temporal validity

\- Environmental conditions

\- Dependency completion

\- Domain rules

\- World integrity



Every evaluation occurs against the current constitutional revision.



\## Constitutional Decisions



Governance may produce one of the following outcomes:



\- Execute

\- Deny

\- Delay

\- Suspend

\- Retry after dependency resolution



Each outcome becomes part of the constitutional history.



\## Constitutional Integrity



Governance preserves:



\- world continuity

\- deterministic execution

\- admissibility

\- dependency consistency

\- replayability

\- evidence preservation



Constitutional integrity is maintained throughout every world revision.



\## Integration Principle



Governance is not executed before simulation.



Governance is executed throughout simulation.



Every Constitutional Process contains constitutional governance.



\## Determinism



Given identical:



\- World Revision

\- Process

\- Constraints

\- Dependencies

\- Tick



Governance produces identical constitutional decisions.



\## Summary



Governance is inseparable from constitutional world evolution.



Every transformation of constitutional state is evaluated before becoming part of the next constitutional revision.



Constitutional simulation therefore governs evolution rather than merely observing it.
