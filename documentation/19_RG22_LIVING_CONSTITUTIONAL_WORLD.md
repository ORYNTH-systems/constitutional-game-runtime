\# 19 — RG22 Living Constitutional World



\## Purpose



This document defines the first executable demonstration of the Constitutional Runtime.



The Living Constitutional World is the reference simulation demonstrating constitutional execution, continuous world evolution, deterministic governance, and replayable constitutional history operating together.



\## Demonstration Objective



Demonstrate a persistent world that evolves through constitutional governance rather than predefined scripts.



The simulation should continue indefinitely while preserving constitutional integrity.

\## Initial Demonstration



The initial world may contain:



\- One region

\- One settlement

\- One population

\- Renewable resources

\- Registered constitutional processes

\- Constitutional governance

\- Replay logging



No scripted story is required.



The world evolves through constitutional dynamics.



\## Observable Behaviors



The demonstration should visibly exhibit:



\- resource growth

\- resource depletion

\- population change

\- autonomous processes

\- governance approvals

\- governance denials

\- scheduled events

\- world revisions

\- replay generation



\## Success Criteria



The demonstration succeeds when observers can verify that:



\- the world evolves continuously;

\- every execution is constitutionally governed;

\- every revision produces replayable evidence; and

\- the simulation remains deterministic under identical initial conditions.



\## Long-Term Vision



Future reference generations expand the Living Constitutional World through additional constitutional processes, larger populations, multiple settlements, richer economies, environmental dynamics, and distributed simulation while preserving the constitutional architecture defined by the Constitutional Runtime Specification.



\## Summary



The Living Constitutional World is the executable proof of constitutional simulation.



It demonstrates that governed execution, constitutional world evolution, and replayable history can operate together within a persistent simulation.
