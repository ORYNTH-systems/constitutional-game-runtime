\# 17 — RG22 World Evolution



\## Purpose



This document defines how constitutional worlds evolve across time.



World evolution is not a sequence of isolated events.



It is the continuous production of constitutional world revisions through governed simulation.



\## Constitutional Principle



A constitutional world is never static.



Every simulation tick may transform the constitutional state.



Every transformation contributes to an ordered constitutional history.

Examples include:



\- Resource availability

\- Population dynamics

\- Environmental change

\- Institutional development

\- Economic activity

\- Migration

\- Cooperation

\- Competition

\- Autonomous behavior



No individual mechanism controls evolution.



Evolution emerges from governed interaction.

Large-scale behavior is not explicitly scripted.

The Constitutional Runtime governs execution.

\## Summary



World evolution is the continuous construction of constitutional history.



Persistent civilizations emerge through governed constitutional processes acting upon an evolving constitutional world rather than through predefined scripts or isolated events.
