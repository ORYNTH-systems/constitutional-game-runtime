\# 13 — RG22 Resource Dynamics



\## Purpose



This document defines how constitutional resources evolve throughout the Living Constitutional World.



Resources are not static inventory values.



They are continuously changing constitutional state variables whose evolution influences governance, execution eligibility, and future world revisions.



\## Constitutional Principle



Every resource exists within constitutional time.



Resources may:



\- grow

\- decline

\- transfer

\- regenerate

\- decay

\- become constrained

\- become exhausted



Every change produces a new constitutional state.



\## Constitutional Resources



Representative resource categories include:



\- Food

\- Water

\- Wood

\- Stone

\- Energy

\- Labor

\- Land

\- Knowledge

\- Wealth



Additional domains may define specialized resources while preserving the same constitutional model.



\## Resource Dynamics



During each simulation cycle resources may experience:



\- Production

\- Consumption

\- Storage

\- Transfer

\- Loss

\- Regeneration

\- Depletion



These transformations occur according to registered constitutional processes.



\## Resource Dependencies



Resources influence one another.



Example:



Food

↓

Population Health

↓

Labor Capacity

↓

Production

↓

Food



Such dependency networks create continuous constitutional feedback.



\## Governance Relationship



Governance evaluates resource availability immediately before execution.



Resource insufficiency may render an otherwise valid action constitutionally inadmissible.



\## World Revision



Every resource transformation contributes to a new constitutional world revision.



Future governance decisions evaluate the updated resource state rather than historical values.



\## Determinism



Given identical:



\- World revision

\- Resource state

\- Registered processes

\- Simulation tick



resource evolution produces identical outcomes.



\## Summary



Resources are constitutional state variables.



Their continuous evolution drives the changing conditions against which constitutional governance evaluates future activity.
