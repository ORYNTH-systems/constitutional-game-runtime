\# 31 — EP-003 Climate



\## Purpose



This document defines Environmental Process EP-003: Climate.



Climate establishes the long-term environmental identity of a constitutional world.



Unlike weather, which changes frequently, climate remains persistent and constrains the admissible range of future environmental evolution.



\---



\# Constitutional Principle



Climate is persistent constitutional state.



It defines the environmental identity from which seasons, weather, water availability, biological productivity, and future environmental processes derive.



Climate is therefore a governing constraint rather than a transient environmental event.



\---

EP-003 is responsible for:



\- Maintaining constitutional climate state

\- Defining long-term environmental identity

\- Constraining seasonal and weather evolution

\- Providing environmental context for all dependent systems

\- Recording immutable climate evidence
