\# 12 — RG22 Simulation Clock



\## Purpose



This document defines the constitutional simulation clock used by the Living Constitutional World.



The simulation clock provides the temporal foundation upon which all constitutional world evolution occurs.



\## Constitutional Principle



Time is a governed constitutional resource.



Every process, event, governance evaluation, execution decision, and world revision occurs relative to the constitutional simulation clock.

\## Tick



A tick is the smallest constitutional unit of simulation.



During a tick the runtime may:



\- execute scheduled processes

\- evolve resources

\- evolve populations

\- evaluate governance

\- execute admissible actions

\- record evidence

\- produce a new world revision

\## Determinism



Given identical:



\- Revision

\- Tick

\- Registered processes

\- Constraints



the simulation produces the same next revision.



\## Constitutional Properties



The simulation clock guarantees:



\- deterministic ordering

\- reproducible execution

\- consistent event timing

\- replayable history

\- stable world progression



\## Summary



The simulation clock is the constitutional heartbeat of the living world.



Every constitutional revision is anchored to an ordered progression of simulation time.
