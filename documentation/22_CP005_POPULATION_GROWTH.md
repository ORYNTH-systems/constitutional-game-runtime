\# 22 — CP-005 Population Growth



\## Purpose



This document defines Constitutional Process CP-005: Population Growth.



Population Growth increases population only when constitutional conditions support long-term viability.



\## Constitutional Principle



Population growth is not automatic.



Growth is admissible only when the world state can sustain additional population.



\## Inputs



CP-005 evaluates:



\- Current population

\- Population health

\- Hunger pressure

\- Food availability

\- Current world revision



\## Outputs



CP-005 may update:



\- Total population



\## Governance Conditions



Population growth is admissible when:



\- population health is stable or optimal;

\- hunger pressure is zero; and

\- food remains available after consumption.



If these conditions are not met, growth is constitutionally denied.



\## Evidence



Each execution records:



\- previous population

\- updated population

\- health

\- hunger pressure

\- food availability

\- governance result
