\# 18 — RG22 Evidence and Replay



\## Purpose



This document defines the constitutional evidence model used by the Living Constitutional World.



Every constitutional world revision produces evidence describing how and why the world evolved.



Evidence is a constitutional property of the runtime rather than a debugging artifact.



\## Constitutional Principle



Every transformation of constitutional state must be reproducible.



No world revision exists without constitutional evidence.

\## Evidence Record



Every constitutional revision records:



\- Revision identifier

\- Parent revision

\- Simulation tick

\- Executed processes

\- Denied processes

\- Governance decisions

\- Constraint evaluations

\- Resource changes

\- Population changes

\- Environmental changes

\- Replay metadata



\## Replay



Replay reconstructs constitutional history.



Replay never guesses.



Replay reproduces every constitutional revision using recorded evidence.



\## Replay Guarantees



Replay produces identical results given identical:



\- Revision history

\- Process ordering

\- Governance decisions

\- Constraints

\- Simulation clock

\## Evidence Properties



Evidence guarantees:



\- replayability

\- auditability

\- deterministic verification

\- constitutional continuity

\- reproducibility

\- historical integrity



\## Relationship to World Evolution



World evolution creates constitutional history.



Evidence preserves constitutional history.



Replay reconstructs constitutional history.



\## Summary



Evidence is the constitutional memory of the runtime.



Every constitutional decision contributes to a replayable history capable of deterministic reconstruction and verification.
