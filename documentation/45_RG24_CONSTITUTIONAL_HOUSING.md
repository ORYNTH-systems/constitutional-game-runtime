\# 45 — RG24 Constitutional Housing



\## Purpose



This document defines RG24-B: Constitutional Housing.



Housing introduces the first infrastructure layer inside the Settlement System.



\## Dependency Chain



Climate → Season → Weather → Water → Soil → Food → Population → Settlement → Housing



\## Constitutional Principle



Housing is not cosmetic world decoration.



It is settlement capacity infrastructure that determines whether population growth can be sustained.



\## Inputs



Housing evaluates:



\- Settlement Stage

\- Settlement Capacity

\- Population

\- Available Wood

\- Available Stone



\## Outputs



Housing updates:



\- Housing Capacity

\- Settlement Infrastructure Evidence



\## Deterministic Rules



Revision 1:



\- Nomadic → housing capacity 100

\- Village → housing capacity 250

\- Town → housing capacity 750

\- City → housing capacity 2500

\- Metropolis → housing capacity 10000



\## Architectural Significance



Housing becomes the first infrastructure constraint connecting resources, population, and settlement growth.



\## Summary



RG24-B establishes housing as a governed constitutional infrastructure state required for future settlement expansion, labor systems, construction systems, and economies.
