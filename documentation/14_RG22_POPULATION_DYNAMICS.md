\# 14 — RG22 Population Dynamics



\## Purpose



This document defines how populations evolve within a Constitutional World.



Population is not a fixed statistic.



It is a governed constitutional state variable that changes continuously through simulation.



\## Constitutional Principle



Every population exists within constitutional constraints.



Population may:



\- grow

\- decline

\- migrate

\- age

\- reproduce

\- fragment

\- consolidate



Every population change produces a new constitutional world revision.



\## Population State



Each population contains constitutional properties including:



\- Total population

\- Age distribution

\- Health

\- Labor capacity

\- Hunger

\- Housing

\- Mobility

\- Settlement membership



These properties evolve over constitutional time.



\## Population Drivers



Population changes arise from constitutional processes such as:



\- Birth

\- Mortality

\- Migration

\- Disease

\- Nutrition

\- Environmental conditions

\- Resource availability

\- Governance decisions



No population transition occurs independently of constitutional state.



\## Population Dependencies



Population influences multiple constitutional resources.



Example:



Food

&#x20;   ↓

Health

&#x20;   ↓

Labor

&#x20;   ↓

Production

&#x20;   ↓

Food



Population therefore participates in constitutional feedback loops.



\## Governance Relationship



Governance evaluates population conditions before approving actions that depend upon available labor, health, settlement stability, or constitutional limits.



Population pressure may render otherwise valid activity constitutionally inadmissible.



\## Constitutional Evolution



Population changes do not occur as isolated events.



They emerge through repeated constitutional simulation cycles and become part of each new world revision.



\## Determinism



Given identical:



\- World revision

\- Population state

\- Resource state

\- Registered processes

\- Simulation tick



population evolution produces identical constitutional outcomes.



\## Summary



Population is a constitutional state variable.



Its continuous evolution shapes future governance, execution eligibility, resource dynamics, and the long-term development of the constitutional world.
